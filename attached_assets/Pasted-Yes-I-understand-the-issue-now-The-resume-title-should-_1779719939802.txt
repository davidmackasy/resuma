Current Problem

The resume title is now aligned with the target job, but the Professional Summary is not written like a real resume summary.

Example bad output:

“Cathodic Protection Field Support Labourer with skills in installation support, equipment maintenance, cable-routing assistance and site quality-assurance inspections...”

This sounds forced and unnatural.

It reads like the AI is trying to summarize the job posting instead of summarizing the applicant.

A resume summary should quickly explain who the applicant is, what experience they have, and what strengths they bring.

It should not sound like a pasted job-description rewrite.

Correct Resume Summary Purpose

The Professional Summary should answer:

Who is this applicant?
What kind of work experience do they have?
What are their strongest transferable skills?
Why do they make sense for this role?

It should be based mostly on the applicant’s actual work history.

It can include job-relevant keywords, but only naturally.

Required Summary Style

The summary should be 3 to 5 short lines.

It should sound human, professional, and grounded.

It should describe the applicant’s background first.

Then connect that background to the target role.

Correct structure:

Line 1:
Describe the applicant’s real experience and background.

Line 2:
Mention strongest work habits, responsibilities, or transferable skills from previous jobs.

Line 3:
Connect those strengths to the target role in a natural way.

Line 4 optional:
Mention reliability, safety, teamwork, customer service, or attention to detail if relevant.

Bad Summary Pattern

Do not write this type of summary:

“[Target Job Title] with skills in [job keyword], [job keyword], [job keyword]...”

This sounds fake when the applicant has not directly held that exact role.

Avoid:

Overloading the first sentence with job-posting keywords
Making the applicant sound like they already worked the exact job if they did not
Repeating the job title too many times
Using awkward phrases like “with skills installation support”
Writing a summary that sounds like a keyword dump
Writing the summary as if it is about the company or job posting

Better Summary Example For This Resume

Use something closer to this:

Professional Summary

Reliable worker with experience supervising frontline staff, managing equipment inventory, completing quality checks, and supporting daily site operations. Strong background in customer service, problem solving, team coordination, record keeping, and maintaining organized work environments. Brings strong attention to detail, safety awareness, and hands-on work habits that transfer well into installation, construction, and field support roles.

This is much better because:

It summarizes the applicant’s actual background
It mentions supervision
It mentions equipment inventory
It mentions quality checks
It mentions customer service
It connects naturally to installation and field work
It does not pretend the applicant is already an expert installer

Summary Generation Logic

The AI should follow this priority order:

Applicant’s actual work experience
Applicant’s actual skills and responsibilities
Applicant’s strongest transferable strengths
Target job keywords only where natural
Final sentence connecting the applicant to the target role

The summary should not lead with the target role unless the applicant already has direct experience in that exact role.

Example:

If applicant has direct installation experience:

“Window and door installer with 3 years of experience...”

If applicant does not have direct installation experience:

“Reliable worker with experience in supervision, equipment handling, quality checks, and site operations...”

This is the correct behavior.
